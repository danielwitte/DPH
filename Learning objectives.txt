
## Learning Objectives

Gain an overview of the different theories, methods and perspectives that are relevant to understanding Diabetes as a Population Health problem.

Understand how Diabetes is approached by different disciplines, at different levels of abstraction.

Obtain an overview of the major sources of evidence that underpin our current understanding of diabetes, its causes, complications and options for prevention and treatment.

Be able to carry out data analyses on synthetic or publicly available data that replicate and expand on selected landmark diabetes trials and observational studies.

Be able to formulate a hypothesis that can improve our understanding of Diabetes as a Public Health problem

Be able to describe the theories, methods and data that could be used to evaluate the formulated hypothesis in practice 

Analyse and critically discuss the relevance and limitations of the proposed theories, methods and data
